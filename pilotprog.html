Our Pilot Program in Tofino

Overview: A Hybrid Renewable Charging Dock

Goal: Test a multi-source, off-grid marine charging dock powered by wave energy, wind turbines, and solar panels to support electric boats and coastal infrastructure in an environmentally sensitive region.

Why Tofino Works Perfectly

  • Consistent Ocean Swells: Ideal for capturing wave energy year-round.
  • Strong Coastal Winds: Average 20–30 km/h in fall and winter to support wind turbines.
  • Summer Solar Potential: Long daylight hours during peak boating season.
  • Eco-Minded Culture: Green tourism hub and community support for sustainability.
  • Remote Marine Operations: Off-grid fishing lodges and whale tour operators benefit directly.

Hybrid System Design

Power Source Role in System Equipment Type
Wave Energy Primary charging power (24/7 motion) Point absorber or surface float generator
Wind Turbine Secondary/backup (esp. winter) Vertical-axis wind turbine (VAWT)
Solar Panels Daylight supplement Marine-grade solar panel array
Battery Bank Energy storage for electric boat charging Lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4)

Smart Controller: Balances power input and regulates charging output in real time.

Pilot Deployment Plan

Site Selection: Small marina with wave/wind exposure—e.g., near Meares Island.

Hardware Installation:

  • 1 wave generator unit (e.g., Eco Wave-style float)
  • 1 quiet, bird-safe vertical wind turbine
  • Solar canopy integrated into dock roof
  • Charging port for 1–2 electric boats
  • Weatherproof monitoring/control system

Potential Partners:

  • Remote Passages or Jamie’s Whaling Station (user testing)
  • District of Tofino and CleanBC (funding and permits)
  • Collaborations with Indigenous communities for stewardship and local employment

Key Pilot Goals

Goal Metric
Validate hybrid energy output kWh generated per source per day
Measure system reliability Downtime, performance, consistency
Track user engagement Charging frequency, time of day, boat types
Compare to grid/diesel costs $/kWh vs. diesel fuel costs
Environmental impact Noise reduction, estimated CO₂ savings

Why This Pilot is Attractive to Funders

  • Promotes green boating and carbon-free marine transport
  • Demonstrates scalable, modular clean tech for coastal regions
  • Supports eco-tourism and Indigenous community employment
  • Reduces ocean noise pollution with quiet electric motors
  • Provides real-world data for renewable energy in marine environments

Target Funding Sources

  • CleanBC Go Electric Program
  • Pacific Economic Development Canada (PacifiCan)
  • Tides Canada or Bullfrog Power community energy grants
  • Indigenous Clean Energy Initiative (for First Nations partnerships)
